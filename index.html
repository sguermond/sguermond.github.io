Hello.

I'm Sarah and I enjoy making things, whether it's building apps to make life easier, writing scripts to streamline a workflow, or designing a tiny automated goat cheese maker.

I graduated in 2012 from the University of Texas at Austin with a BS in Biology, followed by two years of graduate coursework and research in molecular biology, bioinformatics, and statistics at Oregon State University. My research focused primarily on evolutionary adaptations of corals to climate stress.

I teach Python and Ruby at General Assembly. I designed and led a free project-based programming workshop for women through the organization Geek Girls Carrots. In 2015, I was a Rails Girls Summer of Code fellow and I worked on the open-source coding mentorship platform exercism.io.
